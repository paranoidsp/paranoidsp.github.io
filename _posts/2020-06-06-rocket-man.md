* * *
Remember how soundcloud had comments attached to a timestamp?

I'd love to see an interface for that on Youtube too. There's so many iconic
moments in some of my favourite songs ([the drums in In the Air Tonight](https://youtu.be/cuHvKksNriE?t=216)]), for example), that I'd
love to talk to people about and see appreciation for.

## See the appreciation?

There have been so many times I've gone hunting for reviews just to see if
people appreciate the same things that I find in some songs, or to learn and see
how people appreciate things. Some of this is my strong desire to learn what
others appreciate and how they experience things, but I think some of it just
guilty pleasure in seeing something amazing and wanting to see the reactions of
other people appreciating the same thing.

It's a quest to see people's admiration for incredible things, and to show my
own.

## More lists?

A lot of these lists would actually be a lot bigger, but my thoughts on things
are spread out over badly indexed journal files for a few years. So I'm going to
start these lists of with a few examples, and then come back and fill them in
over time.

This list is in no particular order, I'll just keep adding to it whenever I find
a song that I want to publicly admit to liking.

### In the Air Tonight - Phil Collins

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/cuHvKksNriE" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>


Phil Collins starts this off slow, and keeps slowly burning through lyrics, with
a steady background rhythm. Distorted electric guitar sounds, and synthesized
vocals add to a surreal atmosphere that the lyrics take advantage of.

I'm not going to do a full review here, though there might be some of those in
the future, but definitely hang around for the incredible set of drums at [3:36](https://youtu.be/cuHvKksNriE?t=214).


### Rocket Man - Elton John

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/DtVBCG6ThDk" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>

I've been listening to this on repeat again recently, and I'm struck again by
how extremely well the song is crafted. The lyrics, the theme, the music, all of
them hit buttons for me, the same way Bowie's entire Ziggy Stardust album does.

I'd also be remiss if I didn't mention that this was inspired by a Ray Bradbury
short story called [The Rocket Man](https://archive.macleans.ca/article/1951/3/1/the-rocket-man).
